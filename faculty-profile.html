Faculties at INPUMA

Datuk Dr. Anis Yusal Yusoff

Faculty

Courses

ZQA 7018 Leadership

Public Management

Profile Details

Executive Director of INPUMA has been the Former President & CEO of the Malaysian Institute of Integrity, Director General of National Department for Good Governance & Integrity (JITN), Deputy DG of GIACC, PM’s Department, Assistant Resident Representative for United Nations Development Programme (UNDP) and Programme Specialist for United Nations Volunteers (UNV), Bonn, Germany.

Dr. Aslam Abd Jalil

Faculty

Courses

ZQA 7003 Foundation of Public Policy

ZQA 7026 Local/International Public Policy Practice

Profile Details

Aslam Abd Jalil is a migration and displacement studies scholar who is interested in the issues of marginalised communities including refugees, migrants, and stateless persons. An alumni of INPUMA with Master of Public Policy Dr. Aslam had BBA, (Business Administration) from Australian National University and later completed PhD from the University of Queensland, Australia.

Dr. Tariqur Rahman

Faculty

Courses

ZQA 7024 Health System Policy

Profile details

Mohammad Tariqur Rahman - is currently serving as a Deputy Executive Director (Development, Research, and Innovation) of the International Institute of Public Policy and Management (INPUMA). Prof Tariq also served as an Associate Dean (Continuing Education) at the Faculty of Dentistry (UM). Prof Tariq has a wide experience of working in the field of Biomedical Science. In addition to his scholarly publications, Prof Tariq has been a regular contributor in news media expressing his views in various aspects of public interests such as education, health and technology. His opinion pieces are covered in Malaysia and abroad.

Dr. Muhammad Danial Azman

Faculty

Courses

ZQA 7020 Law Governance and Public Policy, ZQA 7025 Gender and Public Policy & ZQA 7014 Research Methodology for Policy Makers

Profile Details

Danial holds a PhD in International Relations (Political Science) from the University of St. Andrews, UK, where he was mentored by the late Professor Ian Taylor, a renowned expert in Africa-Asia Politics and Critical Political Economy. A recipient of the UM Bright Spark Fellowship, he conducted extensive fieldwork across Africa and Asia. Danial earned a Bachelor of Arts (Distinction Honours) in International and Strategic Studies with a minor in East Asian Studies, receiving the Ghazali Shafie Book Prize and Dato Panglima Kinta Yusuf Gold Medal in 2007.

Dr. Sulaiman Mahboob

Adjunct Professor

Courses

ZQA 7019 Public Finance in Policy Making

Profile details

Tan Sri Dato’ Sri Dr. Sulaiman Mahbob, former CEO of the National Recovery Council Secretariat, has extensive experience in public service and corporate roles. He served as Chief Director of the Economic Planning Unit (2006-2009) and was Chief Secretariat of the National Economic Advisory Council during the 1997 Asian Financial Crisis. He was Executive Director of the Malaysian Institute of Economic Research in 1995 and founded the Malaysian Institute of Integrity, serving as its president in 2004. He also chaired Telekom Malaysia (2015) and the Companies Commission of Malaysia (2004-2005).

Guest Lecturers at INPUMA

Dr. Adilah Abdul Ghapor

Guest Lecturer

Courses

ZQA 7014 Research Methodology for Policy Makers

Profile Details

Adilah Abdul Ghapor is a Senior Lecturer at the Department of Decision Science, Faculty of Business and Economics, Universiti Malaya in Kuala Lumpur. She completed her Bachelor's Degree in Mathematical Statistics from Purdue University, Indiana and obtained her Doctoral Degree from Universiti Malaya. Her research interest is on the Statistical Modeling in Mathematical Sciences, focusing on Missing Values, Outliers, and Parameter Estimation. She also works on the application of statistics which also includes socio-economic impact assessment, financial well-being, and issues related with gig economy.

Dr. Asilatul Hanaa Abdullah

Guest Lecturer

Courses

ZQA 7016 History Politics and Development

Profile Details

Asilatul Hanaa Abdullah earned her PhD in History from the International Islamic University Malaysia in 2021, specializing in decolonizing history. Her research interests span politics, public policy history, development, decolonization, gender, and theology, with many of her publications focusing on Islamic history and civilizations. Dr. Abdullah has authored numerous peer-reviewed articles, book chapters, and policy briefs on topics like women’s roles in Islamic societies and legal pluralism in Muslim-majority countries. Her work has gained national and international recognition, earning her invitations to speak at global conferences, seminars, and workshops.

Azizul Amiludin

Guest Lecturer

Courses

ZQA 7017 Economics in Policy Making

Profile details

Azizul Amiludin has served in the financial markets and research industry for twenty years with an 11 year stint at Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM). At BNM, he served in the financial market and investments department including as head of representative office in London. He is also a founding member of the economic research house DM Analytics and asset management firm Redvest. Azizul holds a Bachelors’ degree in engineering from Brown University, Masters in Economics from University Malaya and an LLM in Corporate Finance Law from Westminster University, London.

Assoc. Prof. Dr. Mohd Hafiz Jaafar

Guest Lecturer

Courses

ZQA 7024 Health System and Policy

Profile Details

Assoc. Prof. Dr. Mohd Hafiz Bin Jaafar, an accomplished lecturer, holds an MBBS, MPH, DrPH, and OHD. Specializing in Health Economics and Financing, Health Policy, Hospital Management, Casemix, Economic Modelling, and Econometrics, his research focuses on economic evaluations, casemix analysis, population studies among vulnerable groups, and advancements in digital health and artificial intelligence. He leads the Casemix, Health Economics and Modelling Projection (CHaMP) research group and is affiliated with the Malaysian Health Economic Association, Asia Pacific Academic Consortium for Public Health, and the Malaysian Health Technology Assessment Section.

Professor Dr Maznah Dahlui

Guest Lecturer

Courses

ZQA 7024 Health System and Policy

Profile details

Maznah Dahlui is a Public Health Medicine Specialist who had served for several years with the Ministry of Health, Malaysia and was a General Practitioner before becoming an academician in the University of Malaya in 2007. Her specialty is health economics, with a major interest in the economic evaluation of health programs. She had evaluated many of the country’s health policies and programs, working with various ministries in Malaysia such as Ministry of Health, Ministry of Labor, Ministry of Science and Technology and Prime Minister’s Department.

Dr. Raja Noriza Raja Ariffin

Guest Lecturer

Courses

ZQA 7022 Sustainable Development Policy

Profile details

Raja Noriza Raja Ariffin, a retired Associate Professor from the Department of Political Science, Public Administration, and Development Studies at the Faculty of Business and Economics, UM, is a former Executive Director of INPUMA. With over 20 years of teaching experience in urban management and public administration, her expertise spans public policy, transportation, policy analysis, urban governance, and urban planning. She holds a Bachelor’s in City and Regional Planning (University of Southwestern Louisiana), a Master’s (California State University, Fresno), and a Doctorate in Planning (University of Nottingham, UK).

Dr. Norlaili Abdul Aziz

Guest Lecturer

Courses

ZQA 7024 Health System and Policy

Profile details

Puan Sri Dr. Norlaili Abdul Aziz began her career with the Health Department of Kuala Lumpur City Hall, serving over 10 years in maternal and child health, environmental sanitation, epidemiology, food quality control, and disease control. Awarded an EC-ASEAN fellowship, she studied Food Safety and Hygiene at the University of West of England in 1992. Rejoining the Ministry of Health in 1994, she strengthened food control programs and developed seven food safety guidelines for the 1998 Commonwealth Games. In 2007, she transitioned to academia after two decades as a public health officer.
